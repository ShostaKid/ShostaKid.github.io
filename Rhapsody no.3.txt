<p align="center"><span style="font-weight: 400;">***</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Morning was always Anna&rsquo;s least favorite time of day. Perhaps it was because she loathed the feeling of waking up early, only to realize Sasha had already slipped out of bed. Take this morning, for instance: as the first rays of the new day gently kissed every corner of the room, Anna opened her eyes, only to find her lover gone. The young girl rolled over, sliding out from under the blanket that still held a trace of her girlfriend&rsquo;s warmth, and dragged herself to the bathroom. Today would be a long day; she needed to finish an essay for her professor and draft an outline for the research project she was set to conduct next month.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">&ldquo;Well, at least I have some motivation to get through it. Winter break is next week, and I&rsquo;ll have more time with her,&rdquo; Anna drifted into thought as she stroked her chestnut curls before picking up her toothbrush to begin her morning routine.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Ouch!"</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The cry from the bathroom caught Sasha&rsquo;s attention, sending her rushing from the kitchen to the small room in the corner. She was in such a hurry that she was still holding a pan of half-fried steaks, her apron tied around her waist and spotted with grease. Inside the bathroom, Anna&mdash;clad only in one of Trusova&rsquo;s shirts on top and nothing below&mdash;stood dazed before the porcelain mirror on the wall, her hand still clutching a toothbrush covered in foam.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Anna&hellip;?" Sasha asked, her voice a blend of confusion and concern.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Sasha&hellip;" she whimpered, tears trickling down her cheeks. "I think there&rsquo;s something wrong with my tooth. It hurts&hellip;"</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Carefully setting the pan on the table, Sasha stepped forward, gently cupping the brunette&rsquo;s face before examining it with her phone's flashlight. A dark spot hidden behind the toothpaste foam in Shcherbakova&rsquo;s mouth confirmed her suspicions.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"It&rsquo;s a cavity. You&rsquo;ll have to see a dentist to get it checked," Sasha sighed, then hesitated for a moment before continuing, "And you&rsquo;ll have to go today."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A jolt of electricity ran down Anna&rsquo;s spine as the word "dentist" hit her ears, making her body instinctively shiver.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"I&rsquo;m not going," she shook her chestnut curls defiantly, horrific memories of a previous tooth extraction flashing through her mind. "I have too much work to handle today, and besides, we don&rsquo;t even know if it&rsquo;s a cavity. Maybe&hellip;"</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Precisely because we aren't sure that I&rsquo;m telling you to go," Sasha said sternly. "What if it&rsquo;s something even worse than a cavity? Your health is the priority, Anna."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The redhead then leaned down, softening her voice as she tenderly caressed her girlfriend.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Don't worry, I know a dentist. She&rsquo;s a friend of mine. She&rsquo;ll check it for you and be as gentle as possible. I&rsquo;ll be right there with you the whole time, so please be good this once, Anya?"</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Clearly, Anna didn't care much for Sasha&rsquo;s dentist friend, but the promise of her presence during the exam swayed her. She nodded in silent agreement, going to change while Sasha called the school to report them both absent and scheduled an appointment. Anna could faintly hear the name "Kamila" as Sasha made the call, a name that struck a chord of familiarity. But no matter how hard she searched her mind, she couldn't place who it was. Perhaps she was just a friend of Sasha&rsquo;s she had heard about before? Anna didn't know, and she had no desire to dig deeper. Either way, she&rsquo;d meet her at the clinic soon enough.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"No breakfast?" Anna asked softly as she saw Sasha putting the half-fried meat into the fridge. Last night&rsquo;s "activities" had left Anna&rsquo;s stomach protesting. "I&rsquo;m hungry&hellip;"</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"The doctor told me not to let you eat anything before the exam," Sasha tenderly helped Anna into her fur coat, before leaning down to plant a soft kiss on the cheek that was slightly swollen from the toothache. "Try to endure the hunger for a bit, and I&rsquo;ll take you for a delicious meal afterward, okay?"</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">***</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The scent of medical bandages was the first thing Anna noticed as she pushed open the clinic door, causing her to instinctively shrink back and huddle closer behind Sasha. She hid behind Trusova so thoroughly that the nurse who initially greeted them mistook them for a mother and daughter.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Relax, Anna," Sasha whispered, stroking the soft chestnut hair she so loved&mdash;hair that wasn't as neatly brushed as it usually was for class, but was still enough to enchant her. "Everything will be fine. The doctor seeing you is excellent, and I&rsquo;ll tell her to be very gentle with you. There&rsquo;s nothing to be nervous about."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"I don't want to go in," Anna said quietly, leaning her weight into Sasha as if she wanted to disappear into her arms.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"But you can't just leave a cavity like that, can you? A girl with a rotten tooth isn't very pretty," the redhead smiled warmly. It was rare to see Anna so pouty; she truly seemed terrified of the dentist.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"But&hellip;"</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Anna Shcherbakova, correct?" The nurse suddenly interrupted, approaching them and looking Anna in the eye. "Please follow me; the doctor is waiting inside. And Miss Trusova, here is a list of medications Miss Shcherbakova will need. The clinic&rsquo;s pharmacy is temporarily closed, so you can find another pharmacy at the end of this street."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Medicine already, even before the exam?" Sasha took the list, looking at the nurse in confusion.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"The doctor pre-prescribed some pain relief for Miss Shcherbakova and asked me to give it to you so you could buy it ahead of time. There are also necessary antibiotics and anti-inflammatories."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"I see. Thank you."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"You&rsquo;re not coming in with me?" A small hand suddenly gripped Sasha&rsquo;s sleeve as she made a move to stand up.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"I&rsquo;ll only be gone for a moment," the girl smiled, turning back to pat Anna&rsquo;s head. "Don't worry too much; I&rsquo;m sure Kamila will be gentle with you."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Finally, despite her reluctance, Anna was forced to follow the nurse alone into the room she had been trying so hard not to look at&mdash;the room with the sign "Dentist Valieva" hanging by the door.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The first thing that caught Anna&rsquo;s eye was the pristine white dental chair, with dental tools neatly arranged beside it. They were silver, gleaming under the room&rsquo;s lights. Instinctively, a shiver ran through Anna&rsquo;s body. Just looking at those terrifyingly shiny objects brought back horrific childhood memories&mdash;the time her mother tricked her into thinking they were going out to play only to take her for an extraction, or the time she nearly bit the dentist&rsquo;s hand when he tried to pull her wisdom tooth. To the brunette, anything related to a dentist was nothing short of a personal hell.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Anna Shcherbakova. Welcome to the clinic. I am Dentist Valieva, the head doctor here. Now, sit down so I can perform a quick check-up."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A deep voice spoke, and Anna realized for the first time that someone in a white lab coat was sitting at a desk in the corner, her back to her. It wasn't until she turned around that Anna could see her face clearly. Long, smooth blonde hair, carefully styled, and an elegant but somewhat haggard face, likely from the pressure of work. She was quite attractive, if not beautiful.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">And then, Anya remembered.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">She remembered that fragment of memory.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The memory she had unconsciously recalled when she heard the name "Kamila" but couldn't quite name it.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The memory she had tried to forget.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Because it was far worse than any nightmare about dental tools.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">***</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">It was a freezing winter day in Moscow when Anna woke up in her room, once again finding Sasha gone from her side. At the time, both she and Sasha were still university students in the city. Although they were in different classes, Anna frequently stayed overnight in Sasha&rsquo;s dorm room. Eating, sleeping, playing, studying, and even being intimate&mdash;it all happened in that tiny room. Of course, Sasha didn't live alone; she shared the room with a classmate, Kamila Valieva, a girl from the countryside studying at the medical school of the same university. Because she was a medical student, Kamila had a much more disciplined and scientific lifestyle than her literature-student roommates. Consequently, Anna had almost never seen Kamila. Usually, when she returned to the dorm with Sasha late at night, Kamila was already asleep, and by the time they woke up after a night of passion, Kamila had already headed to the lecture hall. It must also be said that Anna didn't care much about the girl named Kamila. As long as she didn't have a problem with her and her girlfriend&rsquo;s living arrangements, she didn't see the need to find out who she was.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Back to that winter night, when Anna felt cold, hungry, and couldn't find Sasha anywhere in the room. The brunette gently slipped out from under the warm blanket, hesitated for a moment at the pants hanging on the rack, then decided not to wear them. She stepped out into the living room wearing only Sasha&rsquo;s shirt to keep her upper body warm and her underwear below. After all, only the two of them were in the dorm, and Anna was too sleepy for extra steps.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">She went to the kitchen to find some snacks and realized a figure was sitting on the sofa in the living room. The room was dark, and the figure sat so far back that only a messy mop of blonde hair was visible&mdash;looking very much like Sasha&rsquo;s, especially since Trusova hadn't dyed her beautiful hair red yet. Without much thought, Anna moved quietly, like a cat, approaching her, only to realize there was a large bottle of Vodka on the coffee table. &ldquo;What is she doing drinking in the middle of the night without me?&rdquo; Anna frowned slightly, but again, she didn't think twice, moving closer to "Sasha," who was swaying drunkenly and didn't even notice her approach.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Secretly drinking at night like this&mdash;you aren't trying to hide something scandalous from me, are you?" Anna giggled, leaning down just enough for her breath to mingle with the alcohol on the other girl&rsquo;s breath.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">And then, Anna suddenly claimed those lips that always drove her crazy, devouring them like a hungry animal (and she really was hungry), her tongue swirling with the other&rsquo;s, sucking away the last drops of spirits lingering on the corners of her mouth. But there was something very strange: the girl&rsquo;s incredible clumsiness. Granted, Anna had caught her by surprise, but lying there and letting her lead like this didn't feel like the usual Sasha. Her way of kissing was odd&mdash;clumsy and fearful almost simultaneously, as if she had never kissed anyone before. Still, in a half-awake, half-dreaming state, Anna didn't pay much attention to these small details. After satisfying herself, the brunette let go, leaving the girl dazed on the sofa before heading back to the kitchen.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">At that moment, a firm hand suddenly grabbed Anna&rsquo;s waist, spinning her around and pinning her against the wall. Before she could understand what was happening, the girl felt the scent of alcohol on her face, and those moist lips were very close&mdash;so close that Anna was ready to reciprocate the kiss.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">But then, nothing happened. A soft hand slid under the white shirt like a snake, touching bare skin, gliding down and pulling her loose underwear down slightly, but then withdrew as if it had changed its mind, unwilling to further explore the territory it touched every night. The girl quickly ran away toward the bedroom, leaving Anna bewildered and slightly frustrated at being stopped mid-way. But once again, she didn't think much of it. &ldquo;She must be crazy drunk; I&rsquo;ll ask her tomorrow,&rdquo; Anna thought as she went back to the kitchen, put away the snacks, and returned to bed. When she got back, she found Sasha already in bed, curled up in the warm blanket and breathing softly. It didn't take long for Anna to crawl into her arms, nuzzling into her soft chest and falling into an exhausted sleep.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The next morning, when a groggy Anna went down to the kitchen, Sasha was already there, wearing only a crop top and making breakfast for them both.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Good morning, you overslept," Sasha smiled as the familiar long hair appeared at the door.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Good morning," Anna replied dizzily, pulling out a chair and sitting down. "You just woke up too early."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Didn't I wake up early just to make breakfast for you?" Two plates of fried eggs were placed on the table with a loaf of bread. "Here, enjoy."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Thank you." Then, as if suddenly remembering something, Anna looked at Sasha inquisitively. "By the way, where did you go in the middle of the night? And why were you drinking yourself into a stupor in the living room?"</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Sasha&rsquo;s blue eyes blinked for a moment, and her face showed clear confusion. But then, Trusova quickly smiled.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"I did get out of bed in the middle of the night, but only to use the bathroom. As for the person you say was drinking, that wasn't me," Sasha nodded toward the living room sofa. "That was Kamila."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The girl sitting on the sofa, Kamila, looked toward the dining table upon hearing her name. She glanced at Sasha and then at Anna, nodding slightly as if confirming Sasha&rsquo;s words before returning to her book.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Never in her life had Anna felt such a chill down her spine. Now, illuminated by the sunlight, the girl realized that the person sitting on the sofa was identical to the one she had seen dimly in the dark the night before. Unconsciously, the brunette reached up and touched her lips. That kiss, and her bare body, which was meant only for Sasha, had been given by her&mdash;by herself&mdash;to someone else last night. All because of a disastrous mistake, because of her own lack of alertness. And even though this "mistaken exchange" came from confusing her lover with someone else, a strange sensation rose in Anna&rsquo;s gut&mdash;a feeling of ache. The feeling of someone terrified that their wrongdoing would be discovered. She had enough evidence and logic to defend herself. But Anna was still afraid.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Kamila silently watched the couple chat during breakfast. Shortly after, when Sasha saw Anna off, she carefully reminded her to see a doctor if she felt worse (since Anna had claimed a stomach ache to leave early). In that moment, seeing those long chestnut curls fluttering in the wind, the young blonde girl also unconsciously reached up to touch her lips, where the taste of that kiss from the night before still lingered, just a little, from when those chestnut locks brushed against her face in the night&hellip;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">***</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Memories raced like wildfire through Anna&rsquo;s mind. She had forgotten because immediately after that event, Sasha announced that Kamila had moved out of the dorm after receiving a scholarship to MGU. Afterward, Sasha rarely mentioned Kamila, only occasionally bringing her up as a successful former classmate. For that reason, the memory of that story had been buried deep by Anna, and she swore never to tell a soul.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">But now, she was here, alone in a room with that person from years ago. Unconsciously, Anna&rsquo;s body began to tremble uncontrollably. Was that why Kamila asked Sasha to go buy medicine first? So she couldn't come in with her? So Kamila could be alone in the room with her? So&hellip;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"How much longer do you plan to stand there?" Kamila&rsquo;s deep voice spoke again&mdash;not angry, but not gentle either. "Come in and sit down."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Contrary to the girl&rsquo;s fears, Kamila simply picked up a small flashlight and turned toward her as she sat opposite the blonde doctor.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"Open your mouth," Kamila commanded, her voice devoid of emotion. She carefully shone the light inside, the wooden tongue depressor in her other hand moving gently&mdash;enough that Shcherbakova didn't feel uncomfortable&mdash;and frowned slightly upon seeing the cavity. "The condition is quite bad; it's reached the pulp. Doesn't Trusova look after you?"</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The medical stick was tossed into the trash as Kamila turned back and began writing in a notebook on the desk.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">"I&rsquo;ll need to do a more thorough check before deciding how to treat that tooth. Miss Shcherbakova, please lie back in that chair so we can continue the examination."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Anna&rsquo;s heart slowed down a bit. It seemed Kamila wasn't what she feared. It seemed she had forgotten that story, and perhaps she was the only one still haunted by it. For a moment, Anna felt like a fool for overthinking.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">But Anya would never know that in the moment she turned away, Kamila reached up to touch her lips, her heart beating faster than ever.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">And memories, just like with Anna, raced wildly through the doctor&rsquo;s brain.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">But not just the memory of that "transgression."</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">But also memories&hellip;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Between Kamila Valieva and Anna Shcherbakova.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Fragments of memories that perhaps Anya herself didn't even remember.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">But that blonde girl could never forget.</span></p>
