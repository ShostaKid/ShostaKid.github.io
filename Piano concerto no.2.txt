The screen flickered off just as Anna felt her eyelids grow heavy. Drowsiness had slowly overtaken the young girl, leaving her nodding off mid-sentence. The Olympic champion looked up at the nearby clock; it was midnight. Outside the window, a crescent moon embraced the streets of Moscow with its tenderness, while the winds carried the crisp, chilling breath of an approaching autumn. In the northern sky of the ancient city, tiny constellations cast faint glimmers of light, trying to escape the monotony of the night. Those specks of light lingered in her hazel eyes—as if seeking a place to hide, a sanctuary for a young woman's smallest, unspoken desires.

But Anna turned away, glancing instead at her bed, freshly made just days ago. Normally, she could stay up as long as she liked, even until dawn, spending the quiet hours when night truly shrouded Moscow to watch movies or giggle with her best friend over a late-night call. Today was not a normal day. Shcherbakova had already exhausted all the good movies, and her best friend... she was certainly busy now, for tomorrow was the most important day of her life. Anna didn’t want to bother her; the sheer thought of how hectic tomorrow would be was likely already haunting her friend, keeping her from sleep. Anna knew that temperament all too well; after all, she was usually the one to "soothe" her before moments like this.

But this time, Anna didn’t pick up her phone. The words Shcherbakova knew by heart—"Sasha is calling"—did not appear on the screen either. Anna knew why, and she wasn't surprised. After all, for her friend right now, he would certainly be a better comfort than Shcherbakova could ever be.

Lying down on the sheets that still smelled of fresh fabric softener, Anna tossed and turned for a long while, staring at the pale blue ceiling. The girl with oak-brown hair was exhausted after a long day, and she knew she needed to sleep early to prepare for tomorrow morning. Yet, her fatigue-laden brain was the very thing fighting most fiercely against slipping into "that" dream—the one that had occupied her mind for days.

Because Anna knew that dream brought no peace.

Because Anna knew she shouldn't dream like that.

Because Anna knew she was not ALLOWED to dream like that.

Yet, those beautiful long lashes still fluttered shut.

And Anna Shcherbakova dreamt anyway.

Anna Shcherbakova found herself in a grand hall, wearing an exquisitely embroidered brown gown that hugged her silhouette, adorned with diamonds along the hem that spoke of royal nobility. She was at a great palace banquet, her feet gliding past other aristocrats as she searched for something.

Everything felt too real.

The hall was flooded with candlelight and lanterns, illuminating the bustling crowd. Most were nobles from distant kingdoms, radiant and dignified. Some women wore backless gowns that plunged to the waist, revealing graceful curves; others wore voluminous dresses with expensive hats to flaunt their wealth. But no matter how alluring they were, they weren't what the princess sought tonight. After wandering through the vast hall, her gaze finally landed on her.

Alexandra Ignatova—or Sasha, as many affectionately called her—a beautiful lady of the Ignatova bloodline. Her white gown, accented with touches of pink and a cinched waist, accentuated every line of her body. The off-the-shoulder design and delicate threads embraced her slender frame perfectly. Her long golden hair flowed down, veiling her soft, pretty cheeks. She sat alone at the Ignatova table, her gaze distant and her face tinged with a faint, melancholic sadness. Anna caught her breath for a moment. Sasha wasn't dressed provocatively or ostentatiously, yet for some reason, a powerful tremor rose in the princess's heart.

It felt so real she wanted to stay here forever.

The orchestra emerged, playing the first notes of tonight’s Serenade. In the center of the hall, couples began to step out, moving to the rhythm. Anya took a deep breath and made her choice. Two girls dancing together might be unusual, but this was a masquerade ball. Besides, if not now, then when?

"Forgive me, but would the Lady of Ignatova care to join me for a Serenade dance tonight?"

Shcherbakova approached, her hand extended in invitation. Sasha looked surprised, but then a soft smile bloomed, and she took Anna’s hand. Leading her to the center of the floor, the princess bowed to the lady. One hand rested on Sasha's waist, the other clasped her small fingers, and they began to move. In return, Sasha held her hand and looked at her joyfully as they stepped in sync.

Forward, back, and then a spin. They moved as if they were one. Surprisingly, without a single word, their coordination was so seamless that anyone would have mistaken them for a perfect pair.

The melody shifted. The violinist drew his bow faster. Sasha looked at Anna, a fleeting smile on her lips. The princess understood exactly what it meant, even without a word spoken.

Left, right, right, left—one step forward, two steps back. They read each other through a mere glance. Sasha tightened her arm around Anna’s waist, her joy evident in her smile. And the princess felt it too. In her heart, a new Serenade was playing. A Serenade of happiness.

The tempo accelerated as the music reached its crescendo. They remained there, in unbelievable harmony for two people who had "just met." Anna moved forward, Sasha retreated; one moved right, the other left, spinning once before drawing close again. A bead of sweat rolled down Sasha’s temple, dampening her porcelain shoulders, but she didn’t care. If anything, she moved faster. And so did Anna. For the princess, all exhaustion had vanished the moment she held the hand of the one she loved most.

Advance, advance, retreat, right, left, left, spin, and retreat again. As the melody grew more urgent, they moved with increasing speed, as if merging with the music, merging with each other. Before they knew it, the floor belonged to them alone. Their incredible harmony drew the eyes of the crowd; other couples stepped aside, leaving the hall to them. But the two didn't care about the world around them anymore.

Now, their entire universe had shrunk down to the reflection in each other's eyes.

The Serenade ended on a high note. The lady leaned back, and the princess caught her with her left arm. Sasha panted, sweat soaking her white dress, but her eyes shone with pure bliss. Anna was breathless too, her heart racing to the rhythm of the most beautiful Serenade she had ever heard.

Anna woke up abruptly, feeling her heart thumping wildly in her chest. Around her was the lonely silence of Moscow, broken only by the occasional sound of a car on the main road, the mournful cry of nocturnal birds, and the silent sobs trapped in her throat.

"That hateful dream again..."

While Anna tried to soothe her heart—which was vibrating like the frantic strings of a violin—her phone buzzed in the dark, its blue light illuminating a corner of the room. It was a reminder she had set herself.

“Today: Attend Sasha and Makar’s wedding.”
