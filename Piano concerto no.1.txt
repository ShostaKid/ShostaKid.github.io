<p align="center"><em>Allegretto Amore, poi Largo non troppo con Smorzando</em></p>
<p align="center">***</p>
<p>"Hoo… Hoo."</p>

<p>By force of habit, Aleph glanced toward the window. It was the hoot of an owl echoing near his room, signaling the hour when the moon reached its zenith in Argentina's star-studded sky. For reasons unknown, owls had recently begun to frequent the damp, wind-swept mists of Comala, particularly during a summer season like this one. Perhaps they were merely stray birds momentarily perching on the withered branches outside, destined to depart before long.</p>

<p>Or perhaps, they were simply lonely creatures of flight, mirroring the soul of the man with blood-red hair sitting in this darkened room.</p>

<p>Aleph turned his attention back to his desk, his eyes drifting to the clock resting at the corner. The long hand pointed to the one, while the shorter hand touched twelve. Everything around him had long since dissolved into silence, and the counselor himself would have remained plunged into the deep abyss of reflection had he not been abruptly pulled back by the solitary, haunting cry of the owl outside. Yet, Aleph did not let his gaze linger on the clock for long, for right beside it lay an envelope, slightly damp at the edge, adorned with meticulous lines of black ink: <em>"To Aleph, my dear pen pal."</em></p>

<p>The counselor lowered his quill, tracking the shadow of a raven as it sliced across the darkened glass of the window. Darkness flitted through his eyes, as if carrying away everything within them—all of Aleph's innermost thoughts and hidden desires—submerging them into the profound depths of the night outside. He closed his eyes once more, feeling himself dragged down into the bottomless mires of his own mind. They were trying to speak to him, luring him with the sweetest words, caressing the nerves in his head with the echoes of her voice.</p>

<p>Struggling to shake off the thoughts, Aleph stood up, extinguished the oil lamp that still flickered feebly at his desk, and walked toward the bed. Though it was late, the red-haired counselor had no desire to sleep. He was reluctant to lay his back against the mattress just yet, for he knew that what awaited him behind the velvet curtain of his mind was far from comforting.</p>

<p>It was a nightmare. It was a forbidden dream. A dream that <b>ALEPH WAS NOT PERMITTED TO DREAM.</b></p>

<p>Yet, he dreamed nonetheless.</p><h3>***</h3>

<p>From the high window of the castle, Aleph gazed at the landscape unfolding below. Tonight was a night of profound weight. Countless tiny candles, when arranged with meticulous care, could weave a forest of light, illuminating the entire realm. Below, the air was thick with bustle as groups of servants scrambled to put the final touches on the preparations. In the distance, along the winding stone paths, flickering specks of light moved in a hurried procession. They were likely the carriages from neighboring kingdoms, flocking here for the grand gala hosted by the King—his father—to celebrate his eldest daughter’s betrothal to a foreign prince. Well, Aleph cared little for whom his sister wed; he looked forward to this feast for an entirely different reason.</p>

<p>A dry <em>tap-tap</em> echoed before the heavy wooden door groaned open.</p>

<p>"Prince, the hour draws near. Your father sent me to inquire if you are prepared. And as these old eyes can see, it appears your attire still rests upon the bed, and..."</p>

<p>The liquid in the goblet swayed slightly as Aleph turned. It was his butler.</p>

<p>"I am aware, no need for reminders. Everything will be ready momentarily. I crave the utmost privacy while I dress, so pray, grant me a moment alone."</p>

<p>"As you wish, my Prince."</p>

<p>With a bow, the butler retreated, and the door clicked shut. Setting the half-finished wine on the table, Aleph began to don the garments tailored specifically for him. A crisp white shirt and a suit of midnight black, chosen to sharpen the contrast of his blood-red hair. That was all; Aleph had never been one for vanity. Smoothing his somewhat disheveled long locks and misting a hint of fragrance, he surveyed his reflection in the mirror.</p>

<p>"This should suffice," Aleph whispered with a faint smile.</p>

<p>The Great Hall was awash in the glow of candles and lanterns, bathing the boisterous crowd in warmth. Most were nobles from distant lands, draped in splendor and nobility. There were noblewomen in backless gowns that plunged to the hip, flaunting their lithe, alluring waists. Others opted for voluminous dresses that flared grandly, topped with extravagant hats crafted from the finest silks to parade their wealth. Yet, no matter how enchanting or exquisite they appeared, they were not what the Prince sought tonight. The one Aleph yearned for had yet to grace the hall.</p>

<p>The ceremonies—which Aleph found soul-crushingly dull—finally concluded, but not before the butler had to nudge the Prince awake during the King’s speech. Nevertheless, everything proceeded smoothly. His father merely grumbled a few reprimands before drifting off to share laughs with other lords and dignitaries. As for Aleph, he set out to find the one he needed. The part he had anticipated most was about to begin, and he refused to miss it with the woman he loved. After wandering through the vast hall for a time, the object of his search finally appeared.</p>

<p>A white gown, its hem kissed with hints of rose and a bodice that accentuated every curve of her form. The off-shoulder design allowed thin silken threads to embrace her milk-white décolletage. Her long, wood-brown hair cascaded down, veiling her soft, delicate cheekbones. She sat alone at a table reserved for the Borges lineage, her gaze distant, her expression tinged with a faint, haunting melancholy. Aleph’s breath hitched—it felt as though a month had passed in that single second. She was not dressed provocatively like the others, nor was she draped in ostentatious luxury. Yet, for reasons unknown, a powerful tremor of emotion surged within his heart.</p>

<p>The orchestra took their places, drawing the first notes of tonight’s <em>Serenade</em>. In the center of the hall, couples began to drift onto the floor, swaying to the rhythm. Aleph took a deep breath and steeled his resolve. If not now, then when?</p>

<p>"Forgive my intrusion, but would the Lady of House Borges do me the honor of a Serenade tonight?"</p>

<p>He approached her, offering a hand in quiet expectation. Recoleta was startled, but soon a gentle smile bloomed on her lips as she placed her hand in his. Leading her to the center of the hall, where other couples moved in harmony, he bowed before her. One hand rested at her waist, the other clasped her slender fingers, and they began to move as one. In response, Recoleta held his hand and looked at him with a joy that mirrored his own as they stepped into the rhythm.</p>

<p>Forward, back, and then a whirl. They seemed to dissolve into one another within the dance. Miraculously, though not a word was exchanged, they synchronized so effortlessly that any onlooker would have mistaken them for a lifelong pair.</p>

<p>The melody shifted. The violinist drew his bow with increasing fervor. Recoleta looked at Aleph, a fleeting smile gracing her lips. He understood its meaning perfectly, though she remained silent.</p>

<p>Left, right, right, left—one step forward, two beats back. They read each other’s souls through a mere lock of eyes. Recoleta tightened her hold slightly around Aleph’s shoulder, her delight radiant in her smile. And he felt it too. Deep within him, a new Serenade was playing. The Serenade of happiness.</p>

<p>The tempo accelerated as the music climbed toward its crescendo. They remained there, an unbelievable harmony existing between two souls who had only just met. He pressed forward, she yielded; he moved right, she veered left, spinning once before drawing close. A bead of sweat rolled down the lady’s temple, dampening her porcelain shoulders. But Recoleta did not falter; if anything, she quickened her pace. And so did Aleph. For the Prince, all exhaustion vanished the moment his hand felt the delicate touch of the woman he cherished most.</p>

<p>Step, step, back, right, left, left, spin, and back again. As the melody grew more urgent, they moved faster. It was as if they were merging with the Serenade, merging with each other. Without realizing it, the stage had become theirs alone. Their incredible synergy drew the eyes of the crowd, and one by one, the other couples drifted to the sidelines, leaving the hall to them. But the two of them no longer cared for the world outside.</p>

<p>For now, their entire universe had narrowed down to the reflection in each other's eyes.</p>

<p>The symphony ended on a piercing high note. The lady leaned back in a graceful arch, supported by the Prince’s arm. She panted softly, sweat soaking into her white dress. Yet, her eyes shimmered with pure bliss. And so did his. His breath was ragged, and his heart hammered to the beat of the most joyous Serenade Aleph had ever heard.</p>

<p>"Thank you, My Lady, for that dance," Aleph said, struggling to steady his breath as he approached Recoleta. She stood alone at the corner of the corridor, gazing once more into the void. "It was the most magnificent dance of my life."</p>

<p>"The honor was mine, Prince," Recoleta smiled, offering a slight tilt of her head. "Everything just now felt like a dream. However, the hour has come, and I cannot stay any longer. Farewell, Prince."</p>

<p>But just before she turned away, the Prince caught her hand. It was small, fitting perfectly within his palm as he squeezed it gently.</p>

<p>"Do not depart in such haste," Aleph pleaded. For a moment, he felt as though he had lost every word he had ever learned from the scholars. "Please... stay with me, just a little longer..."</p>

<p>Aleph tried to look directly into those rose-colored eyes, searching for a spark of the happiness he had seen moments ago when they whirled to the rhythm of a <em>Serenade</em> overflowing with emotion. But contrary to his hopes, those depths were now filled only with sorrow. Recoleta quickly turned her face away, avoiding the Prince’s gaze.</p>

<p>"I am sorry, but I truly cannot," Recoleta replied, her voice dropping to a whisper as her small hand struggled to free itself from his loving grip. "I understand what you desire, but we... we simply cannot be. Forgive me, Prince. And... please, forget me."</p>

<p>Gentle yet resolute, she untwined his fingers one by one before vanishing toward the door. She left the red-haired Prince standing at the threshold, his arm still outstretched in a hollow, desperate wish to hold onto a fading shadow.</p>

<p>"Don't go... please, don't go..."</p><h3><b>***</b></h3>

<p>Aleph jolted awake in the sweltering heat of a Comala summer, feeling sweat trickle slowly down his temples. He glanced at the pendulum clock in the room—which ticked with an agonizingly slow rhythm—and realized it was only two in the morning. The counselor had barely slept for an hour, and that wretched dream had decided to deny him even a single minute of respite.</p>

<p>The red-haired man slowly placed a hand over his chest, feeling the thumping within his body. It was steady, powerful, and frantic—much like the rhythm of the Serenade in his recent dream. Only here, there was no radiant hall, no dancing couples, and no Recoleta. Here, there was only him and a heart still beating to the lingering taste of a dream that was as beautiful as it was haunting.</p>

<p>After a long silence, the counselor rose from his stone-hard bed and walked to the desk, lighting the oil lamp. This was how he usually spent his nights—processing stacks of documents or answering inquiries from across the globe. But tonight was different. Aleph wished to push all of that aside and pull out a small piece of parchment to pen a reply to her. He dipped his quill into the inkwell—a habit he had developed since knowing her—and began to carefully trace lines of ink upon the parchment, which glowed yellow under the lamplight.</p>

<p><em>"Dear Recoleta,</em></p>

<p><em>No words can capture the joy and happiness I felt upon receiving your letter these past days. It is wonderful to know that your life has found its stability and that you have discovered what you sought out there. I truly wish to ask more about the things you have seen, the experiences you have gathered, and the wisdom you have woven into the pages of your own books. But I believe that knowing you are happy and healthy is what I truly need to hear most. After so long without seeing you, that is all that matters. As for the rest, I trust we will have many more letters and much more time to share everything you wish to say.</em></p>

<p><em>Regarding your invitation in the letter, I must truly express my regret that..."</em></p>

<p>The quill abruptly stopped. The counselor bit his lip. What was he thinking? She had written this letter with her own hand, truly wishing to see him there. Was he really going to let some trivial, lingering emotion ruin the joy of someone he cared for? A wise counselor, a seeker of the Transcendental, would never do such a thing. And Aleph? He was <b>NOT</b> permitted to do so.</p>

<p>He struck through the words <em>"express my regret"</em> and wrote instead: <em>"It is a profound honor to be present at such an occasion, for a wedding is undoubtedly the most momentous event in a woman’s life. As your devoted friend, I would be delighted to attend..."</em></p>

<p>But then, Aleph struck through those lines as well. He didn't want to. He didn't want to. He <em>didn't</em> want to.</p>

<p>He wrote again. Then crossed it out. Wrote. And crossed it out.</p>

<p>Outside, the moon struggled to cast its feeble light through the thick clouds that smothered Comala in darkness. An owl let out a low hoot, drifting endlessly toward the south, sounding like a sigh from the night itself as it felt the suffocation of every living creature beneath its shroud today.</p>

<p>In the small office, under the flickering oil lamp, the red-haired man let a heavy blot of black ink fall onto the parchment—dark and stagnant, much like his own mind in that very moment.</p>
