<p>"Are you alright?" Anaxa asked, his voice a low, resonant hum. His gaze seemed anchored to the lacerations marking her ivory skin.</p>


<p>"Young Master?" Castorice looked up, her eyes wide with startled recognition. Anaxa immediately caught the shimmer of unshed tears pooling in her lashes. Realizing she had exposed her vulnerability, she jerked her head away, her frame still racked with tremors following the ordeal. "I am... I am fine," she rasped. "Please, do not trouble yourself for me. You should return to your chambers before your father finds you here."</p>


<p>Initially, the sight of Castorice in such a state had ignited a cold, searing fury within Anaxa—a profound anger toward his parents for their senseless cruelty. But as he looked closer at the weeping wounds and her frail, shivering form, his rage ebbed into a deep, aching pity for the small maid.</p>


<p>"Come," Anaxa whispered, reaching down to gently clasp her swollen, bloodied hand. He squeezed it with a tender firmness. "Come to my room. I have bandages and medicine there. I will tend to you."</p>


<p>"No, Young Master, you mustn't!" Castorice gasped, her breath hitching. She recoiled as if his touch were a brand of fire, stumbling back a step. She winced as the sudden movement pulled at the raw, angry welts on her back. "If your father knew... he would punish us both. He would think I was... enticing you, or worse! Please, just leave me. I will manage on my own."</p>


<p>Anaxa’s expression hardened, a deep furrow appearing between his brows. He stood to his full, imposing height, his shadow casting a protective shroud over her.</p>


<p>"My father will not know," he said, his voice dropping to a resolute whisper. "And if he dares accuse you of anything, he will have to answer to me. Besides, you are in no state to care for yourself. Look at you, Castorice. You can barely stand."</p>


<p>He stepped closer, his resolve unwavering. He saw the sheer terror in her eyes, a fear born from years of enduring his parents' unyielding malice. He knew that fear was a deeper wound than any lash of a whip.</p>


<p>"I am not asking for your permission," Anaxa stated, leaving no room for argument. He slid an arm around her waist, careful to avoid the worst of her injuries, while the other guided her arm over his shoulder. Her small, thin body felt incredibly fragile against him, and he could feel the rapid, staccato vibrations of her trembling. The linen of her dress, stained and damp with blood, felt cool and wet against his skin.</p>


<p>"Lean on me," he murmured, his warm breath ghosting over her ear. He began to move, slowly, deliberately, leading her away from the servant’s quarters and toward the opulent, dimly lit wings of the main mansion.</p>


<p>Castorice fought him no longer. The pain was a rhythmic hammer beating against her back, her legs felt like water, and the sheer safety of his presence was a gravity she could not resist. She leaned her forehead against the solid muscle of his shoulder, closing her eyes as a fresh wave of tears escaped her lashes. She felt humiliated, terrified—and yet, for the first time since the beating, she felt a flicker of hope. Small, but enough to cling to.</p>


<p>They reached his door. Anaxa nudged it open with his foot, guided her inside, and clicked it shut behind them. He brought her to his grand four-poster bed and helped her sit, every movement measured and soft.</p>


<p>"Stay still," he commanded. He went straight to an ebony nightstand and produced a small, locked mahogany box. As he fumbled with the key, Castorice watched him, huddled on the edge of the plush velvet mattress. The bright lamps of his room cast a warm halo, briefly banishing the shadows of the hallway. The box clicked open, revealing tinctures, white cloth, and rolls of clean linen.</p>


<p>"This will sting," he warned, returning to her side with a cotton swab in hand. His eyes, usually sharp and playful, were now grave and clouded with worry. "But it is necessary."</p>


<p>Anaxa helped her turn, careful not to move her more than required. As he pulled the tattered remnants of her dress away from her skin, a sharp, involuntary intake of breath escaped his chest. It was far worse than he had imagined. Crisscrossing welts of deep crimson marred her pale back; some had split open, weeping a mixture of clear fluid and fresh blood. The skin was angry and inflamed, a silent testament to the violence she had endured.</p>


<p>He took a slow, deep breath to steady his hands. He uncorked a bottle of dark antiseptic—a pungent, colorless liquid.</p>


<p>"Stay with me, Castorice," he whispered, his voice a low, comforting hum.</p>


<p>The moment the soaked cotton touched the first laceration near her shoulder blade, Castorice went rigid. A sharp, stifled cry tore from her throat. Her fingers gripped the heavy bedspread so hard her knuckles turned white. She bit her lower lip, struggling to smother the whimpers.</p>


<p>"I know, I know it hurts," Anaxa murmured, working with quick precision. He leaned close to her ear. "It will be over soon. Just breathe, Castorice. In and out. I am here with you."</p>


<p>She anchored herself to his voice, to the warmth of his breath on her skin. The antiseptic felt like liquid fire, scouring the dirt and the lingering ache of the whip. When he finally withdrew the swab, she slumped forward, exhausted and trembling. Anaxa waited for her to catch her breath before applying a soothing healing salve—a thick herbal ointment that felt cool and slick against her burning skin.</p>


<p>As he worked, the silence between them was heavy yet intimate. He was acutely aware of the delicate curve of her neck and her shallow, ragged breathing.</p>


<p>"Why?" he finally asked, his voice full of confusion. "What was it this time? They do not usually... they do not go this far."</p>


<p>Castorice flinched, but the physical pain had subsided enough for her to speak. "I... I accidentally broke a ceramic statue on the mantel in the drawing room," she whispered, her voice thick with shame. "The one with the little gilded swans. I was dusting, and my hand slipped. Your mother saw it shattered on the floor."</p>


<p>Anaxa’s grip on the bandage tightened. A ceramic swan. That was the measure of his parents' "humanity."</p>


<p>He did not answer after that. He simply continued his work, wrapping a clean length of linen around her torso to secure the gauze. It was a clumsy process, his fingers frequently brushing against the soft curve of her ribs. When he was finished, he stepped back to survey his work.</p>


<p>"It is done," he said, his satisfaction mingled with a simmering resentment. "Now, you will stay here tonight."</p>


<p>Castorice shook her head immediately. "No, Master. You cannot. If I am not in my bed in the servant’s wing, they will know something is wrong. It will only make things worse."</p>


<p>"And if you go back, you will lie on those wounds and risk infection," he countered stubbornly. He sat beside her, gently cupping her face with both hands, forcing her to look into his sincere blue eyes.</p>


<p>"Listen to me," he said, his voice dropping as if sharing a secret. "You will not return to your room. You will sleep right here. I will take the floor. In the morning, before the sun rises, I will take you back. My parents do not wake until noon. No one will know."</p>


<p>He saw the fear, the doubt, and a profound, reluctant gratitude battling in her eyes. Finally, she relented, a single tear tracing a path down her cheek.</p>


<p>"Thank you, Young Master," she murmured, her formality a habit she couldn't break. "You are... too kind."</p>


<p>He brushed the tear away with his thumb.</p>


<p>"My name is Anaxa," he corrected softly. "And I am not kind. I am simply tired of standing by while you suffer."</p>
