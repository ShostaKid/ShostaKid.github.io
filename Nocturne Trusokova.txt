<p>"You’re special, you know."</p>

<p>"Hey, are you still here with me, Anneshka?"</p>

<p>"Would you like to dance?"</p>

<p>"What?" This time, it was Sasha’s turn to be bewildered.</p>

<p>"Well, you wondered how I can dance to 'boring' melodies, so I want to show you. And what could be better than a tedious explanation? Experiencing it for yourself, I suppose."</p>

<p>For a fleeting second, a war raged in Sasha’s mind. On one hand, she understood Anna’s point. If she had to sit through a forty-minute lecture on the role of music in figure skating, she’d likely pass out from boredom. Experience was the fastest teacher, after all.</p>

<p><em>Let’s just say it’s because she didn't want Anna to stand there looking like an idiot.</em></p>

<p>"I should warn you, I don't know any of those royal, aristocratic dances of yours. Don’t blame me if I accidentally step on your feet," Sasha joked, though her lips were so dry she instinctively licked them.</p>

<p>"Don’t worry. Just let your body relax and listen to the rhythm. I believe you’ll find it without any instruction," Anna smiled, seemingly oblivious to her roommate’s tension, as she hit play on her phone. "Let the music guide your every movement."</p>

<p>Anna turned to face Sasha and extended a hand, waiting. In that moment, Sasha realized just how much smaller Anna was—half a head shorter, a perfect distance for Sasha to catch the scent of Anna’s hair. But that certainly wasn't why her heart was hammering out of sync.</p>

<p>Sasha took a slow breath.</p>

<p>Then came the violin. Still a quarter note in C minor, vibrating as the player drew the bow long across the strings. Long enough to stretch the music’s duration. <p>The violin paused for a fraction of a second, just enough for Sasha to close her eyes and inhale. She could see the endless darkness stretching toward the North Pole, the heavy atmosphere of the ancient city on the Neva, and the crescent moon embracing it all. And here, in this small hotel room during training camp, everything was being recreated perfectly. <p>Sasha opened her eyes and realized her "rival’s" fingers were woven through hers, holding on with just enough strength to radiate warmth. When had that happened? Sasha didn't care anymore. <p>It wasn't a perfect hand. But it was a real one.</p>

<p>So real that Sasha only wanted to hold it tighter, to sear the memory of it into her mind.</p>

<p>"Cheater."</p>

<p>"Look who’s talking—the one who started this situation in the first place."</p>

<p>Sasha didn't reply. Instead, she half-closed her eyes and continued to move. Following the music, following the beat of her heart. Forward, back, a slight drift to the right, then left as the bow changed direction. She pulled Anna closer—closer to herself. To feel the steady pulse of the piano accompaniment. To feel it beating inside Anna’s chest. To feel the warmth of the melody still being played.</p>

<p>The violin drew its final stroke, merging with a high note from the piano. They became one, as the breath of the two girls met in a long, lingering resonance.</p>
