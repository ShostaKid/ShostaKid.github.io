<p align="center"><em>Largo amoroso, Sotto Voce e Smorzando</em></p>
<p align="center">***</p>
<p>Kamila wept. Her tears traced paths down her porcelain cheeks—scalding, salt-laden, as bitter as the deep. They fell upon the ship’s deck, seeping into the moss-clung timber.</p>

<p>“Why did you not choose me? Why did you not recognize me? Why did you choose him?”</p>

<p>Despair was a cold weight in her chest. The mermaid gazed at her legs—exquisite, slender, and pale. But what use were these limbs now, when Princess Scherbakova had walked away with another? Kamila had traded her celestial voice for these feet, for legs were the only bridge to her princess. Yet now, with the distance closed, her silence became the new chasm. To gain one was to lose the other, leaving her with nothing to reach the one she loved. Was love truly this arduous? Was fate this insurmountable, despite all she had sacrificed? Kamila wanted to scream, to wail until the sorrow left her body, but she had no voice left to give.</p>

<p>Suddenly, a blade surged from the depths, clattering onto the deck before her. Startled, Kamila rushed to the railing. Below, her sisters rose from the waves.</p>

<p>“Beloved sister!” they cried. “We have bargained with the Sea Witch. If you pierce the heart of the princess with that knife, your tail shall return, and you may come home to the sea. Otherwise, you shall dissolve into foam should she fail to love you. Hurry, sister! The dawn draws near! Return to where you belong. Return to your life!”</p>

<p>Kamila looked up. The sky was pale with the first tremors of light; stars were surrendering to the morning. Tears fell once more. Was she to slaughter the one she loved? If not, she would perish in a foreign world, wasting the heavy price her sisters had paid. What was the right path? What was left for her?</p>

<p><em>Did she even wish to live?</em><br />
She could survive, but she would be a hollow shell without love.<br />
To live, yet to be empty.</p>

<p>Kamila wiped her eyes and took up the knife. It was a masterpiece, a slender hilt of iridescent pearl, a blade of polished shell, so sharp it seemed to cut the very air. With a final, grim resolve, she set off.</p>

<p>Little Kamila Valieva glided through the crowd, a vision of haunting beauty. Her blonde hair was as soft as silk, her hands as delicate as sculpted jade. She moved with the silent grace of a cat, her skin glowing like pearl against her sea-algae green gown. Wherever she passed, the air seemed to shimmer, yet her face bore a melancholy as deep as the ocean floor. The guests were mesmerized; even Princess Anna Scherbakova felt her gaze pulled away from Prince.</p>

<p>The mermaid stood before her princess, locking eyes with her. Anna felt a sudden, inexplicable tremor. Kamila’s eyes were the deep, endless blue of the abyss; her lips were red as blood, her features sharp and otherworldly. In contrast, Anna had eyes of dark obsidian, chestnut hair, and the soft, rounded ears of humanity. They were worlds apart, yet Kamila loved her with a frantic, agonizing intensity, aching to scream her devotion. But alas, she could not speak anymore!</p>

<p>“I love you.” For the first and final time, Kamila spoke the words in the silence of her soul.</p>

<p>Then, Kamila raised the pearl knife and plunged it straight into Anna’s abdomen. Cold and effortless, the blade bit deep. Chaos erupted as Anna Scherbakova buckled, clutching her bleeding wound. Those dark eyes Kamila adored widened, mafirst in shock, then in absolute terror. But before the guards could reach them, Kamila lunged, pushing Anna over the railing and leaping after her into the void. She held Anna tight, desperate to feel the warmth she had always craved.</p>

<p>Dawn. The first rays of gold struck them. The mermaid pressed her ear to the princess’s chest. A heart still beat—thumping weakly, fading fast. There was no time left.</p>
<p>As they hit the water, the light chased them down, piercing the frigid blue. Kamila’s legs dissolved; her body began to shatter into a thousand bubbles. She pulled Anna into one last embrace, pressing a final, desperate kiss against those fading lips.</p>

<p>But there was nothing left.</p>
