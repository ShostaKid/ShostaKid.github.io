<p align="center"><em>Adagio Lugubre</em></p>
<p align="center">***</p>
<p>“Why… why did you leave me all alone?”</p>

<p>Recoleta, her trembling arms stained with crimson, cradled Aleph’s dying frame against her chest. Tears mingled with blood, cascading onto the pale, fading skin of the man she loved. The final embers of his warmth flickered against her. With her brown hair draped over him and her arms tightened in a desperate attempt to tether him to this world, she sobbed amidst the freezing rain.</p>

<p>“Don’t… don't go. Please, don’t leave me...”<br />
***<br />
“Have you been waiting long?”</p>

<p>Recoleta’s voice chirped with joy as she drew near. Clad in the red sweater Aleph had gifted her for her recent birthday, her brown hair dusted with snowflakes that mirrored the porcelain skin of her beloved, she looked like a vibrant bloom amidst the grey, bustling crowd.</p>

<p>“I’ve only just arrived,” Aleph smiled, a gentle tilt of his head sending a flurry of snow dancing from his red locks. “It’s freezing today, yet you’re dressed so lightly. It’s snowing, you know.”</p>

<p>“It’s fine, really. The walk here warmed me up. Besides,” Recoleta suddenly threw her arms around him, burying her face into his broad chest, “being in your arms is warmer than any coat could ever be.”</p>

<p>But then, to her utter bewilderment, he gently unraveled her embrace.</p>

<p>“What… what’s wrong?” she asked, her voice a fragile mix of surprise and confusion.</p>

<p>“I’m sorry,” Aleph replied. His tone remained soft, yet Recoleta could feel an icy chasm opening between his words. “The truth is, I asked you here to tell you something important.”</p>

<p>And then, he said it. He spoke with a tone so nonchalant, as if it were a cosmic inevitability—a truth as plain as the Earth revolving around the Sun. It was so clinical, so cold, and so merciless that Recoleta found herself shivering, though she knew it wasn’t from the winter air.</p>

<p>“Let’s end this. We’re over.”</p>

<p>“What…?” Recoleta’s sapphire eyes widened, shattered by disbelief.</p>

<p>“You heard me,” Aleph said, a faint shadow of sorrow flickering in his gaze.</p>

<p>“Why… why would you want this? Did I… did I do something wrong?” she stammered, her entire body vibrating with the rhythm of her racing heart.</p>

<p>“Don’t look at me like that,” Aleph turned away with a heavy sigh. “The reason? It’s simple—we no longer fit. That’s all. There’s nothing deeper to it. I feel the spark between us has withered over time, and I don’t want to force you, or…”</p>

<p>“Force me? How could it be forcing me?” Recoleta’s voice rose, fueled by a growing agitation. Inwardly spiraling, she lunged forward, gripping his hands tightly. “Is it because of him, Aleph? Is it Merlin? Do you want to be with him more than me? What did I do wrong? In what way am I found wanting compared to him? Tell me, Aleph. TELL ME NOW!”</p>

<p>Her voice escalated into a scream; she was unraveling. Her frame shook with every word. Passersby began to linger, drawn by the spectacle. Yet, Aleph’s demeanor remained unchanged. He detached her hands from his coat—gently, but with a terrifying finality.</p>

<p>“Because my feelings are no longer what they once were. I believe it is unfair to let you be the only one pouring your heart into this relationship. Breaking up is the kindest path. Do not bring Merlin into this; he has nothing to do with it. I know you are going through a difficult time, and I… I don’t believe I can fill that void in your soul anymore. I am sorry for causing you pain. I am sorry for wounding you, but it is done. Farewell, and I wish you luck with whoever comes next. I hope you forget me soon.”</p>

<p>Amidst the falling snow, Aleph walked away without a single backward glance. He left Recoleta standing there, a solitary figure as the white flakes settled upon her brown hair, radiating a cold that went deeper than the marrow of her bones.<br />
***<br />
“Why… why did you leave me?”</p>

<p>It was past half-past eleven. Recoleta sat alone at the bar. The burn of the liquor failed to numb the reality; instead, it dragged her deeper into an abyss of endless thought.</p>

<p>You said you didn’t want me to feel cheated in this relationship, but don’t you know? When you left, that was when I lost everything.</p>

<p>I gave the bloom of my youth to love you, to wait for you, to care for you. My love for you outweighs the world itself. But did you ever truly understand?</p>

<p>The liquid in her glass shimmery as she raised it to eye level.</p>

<p>I only wanted us to be happy. I promised to bring you joy. Why were you in such a hurry to leave me behind in this wasteland?</p>

<p>Why? All I ever wanted was your heart. Is even such a small thing too much to ask?</p>

<p>You said your feelings had changed… but have you truly forgotten everything we shared?</p>

<p>Images flickered like a broken film strip in her mind: the first time they spoke, the letters they spent hours crafting for one another, the red sweater he picked out himself, the "misshapen" stuffed animal he had knitted for her, the domestic bliss of cooking together in their cozy apartment, and the way he whispered her name while holding her fragile form under the glow of a full moon.</p>

<p>These memories struck her like physical blows. Unable to bear the weight, her emotions broke through in the form of hot, silent tears that traced paths down her flushed cheeks. They sparkled under the dim bar lights, heavy with a grief that had no bottom.</p>

<p>***</p>

<p>“Excuse me, miss, it’s past midnight.”</p>

<p>The waiter’s voice sounded like a distant echo, pulling her consciousness back from the haze.</p>

<p>“The bar is closing, so if you could…”</p>

<p>“No need to say more. I understand. Thank you,” she rasped, her voice thick with alcohol as she rose unsteadily from her stool.</p>

<p>“Shall we call a cab for you? It’s raining quite hard and you look…”</p>

<p>“No need. Thank you.”</p>

<p>The waiter was right; the sky had opened up in a torrential downpour. But the storm outside was nothing compared to the deluge within her soul. The heavy droplets lashed at her face, yet she felt nothing but the increasing weight in her heart.</p>

<p>In the white-out of the rain, Recoleta stumbled forward, wandering aimlessly into the dark…<br />
***<br />
Ding-dong.</p>

<p>The chime of the doorbell at such an hour pulled Aleph’s attention away from his monitor. Who could it be at this time? he wondered. When he pulled the door open, his surprise turned to shock. Standing before him was a figure drenched from head to toe, strands of hair matted against her face by the rain.</p>

<p>“Aleph…” she started.</p>

<p>“Is that you? Why are you here so late? Is something wrong? You’re soaked! Come inside and dry off,” Aleph said, swinging the door wide to usher her in.</p>

<p>“I… I don’t need to. There is something… one thing I must say right now…”<br />
***<br />
The rain grew more violent.</p>

<p>And Aleph’s lifeblood flowed more freely.</p>

<p>The warmth of her beloved was slowly slipping through her fingers. Perhaps it was the freezing rain, or perhaps it was because Aleph had ceased to breathe. Who could tell?</p>

<p>Recoleta knew only one thing. One final truth she had to whisper to him.</p>

<p>She raised the knife, leveling it with her gaze.</p>

<p>“I won’t let you go alone. For even death shall not tear us apart.”</p>

<p>I love you.</p>

<p>Do you have any idea how much I love you?</p>

<p>I know I am selfish, but it is simply because I love you too much.</p>

<p>Because I want to be bound to you until the very end of the road.</p>

<p>Because I want to hold you in these arms just once more.</p>

<p>Because I want you to belong to me—and only me—forever.</p>

<p>Because I won’t let you leave me behind again.</p>

<p>I’m sorry…</p>
