“Shoot me.”</p>

<p>The gun’s cold trigger rested in the commander’s hand. All she had to do was squeeze once more.</p>

<p>Just once.</p>

<p>Iglika closed her eyes. The winter chill burned her lungs as she inhaled.</p>

<p>But she couldn’t do it.</p>

<p>The trigger stayed where it was—cold, unmoving.</p>

<p>No shot rang out. No flash of fire.</p>

<p>“Shoot me, Iglika,” the man rasped, his voice breaking from the pain of open wounds. “Shoot me, and it all ends here.”</p>

<p>“I can’t,” her own voice had grown hoarse at some point—from the cold, or from something else? Even she no longer knew. “I can’t do it, Pyrrhos.”</p>

<p>“Yes, you can. You’re the commander of an armed force. You’ve pulled the trigger and killed countless others guilty against the revolution. You can shoot me, Iglika. You have to.”</p>

<p>“You’re not like them.”</p>

<p>“I’m not like them—because I am them. I betrayed the revolution. I went against everything I promised. I acted against my own beliefs. I… betrayed your trust. So I have to die. Shoot me, Iglika.”</p>

<p>“Everyone makes mistakes. And mistakes can be fixed.”</p>

<p>“Not betrayal, Commander. You know that better than anyone. ‘Once faithless, forever untrusted.’ I’ll never be worthy of trust again.”</p>

<p>A tear rolled down, but the winter cold quickly froze it on her soft skin.</p>

<p>BANG.</p>

<p>The dry crack of the gunshot shattered the silent winter night. It pierced through the falling snow, shook the bare trees, and sent a faint ripple across the icy surface of the Danube—like a small, anguished cry.</p>

<p>And it broke Iglika’s composure completely.</p>

<p>Blood poured—far too much. This wasn’t what she had planned. She had only meant to shoot near his chest, enough to knock him unconscious without killing him, so she could drag him to the field hospital. But Pyrrhos’s last-second twist sent the bullet straight into the vampire’s artery.</p>

<p>“Damn, you really are one hell of a shot, Iglika,” the soldier managed through labored breaths, one hand clutching the wound. Blood soaked through his green shirt. “But like I always said—you’re still so naive, human.”</p>

<p>“Lie still—I’ll find a way to save you right now—” For the first time, Iglika heard her own voice tremble like that. Her movements were frantic, her mind in chaos. Her hands were drenched in fresh blood as she tried to press the wound closed.</p>

<p>But it kept flowing—too much.</p>

<p>“Stop wasting your energy, Iglika. Look at me.”</p>

<p>“The field hospital—”</p>

<p>“LOOK AT ME.”</p>

<p>Those blood-red vampire eyes held no hunger now—only sorrow, gentleness, and the slow fading of life.</p>

<p>“Do you remember this place? Where we last met.”</p>

<p>“I remember.”</p>

<p>“I wanted—fuck this shit—I wanted to tell you everything. But I couldn’t. I wanted to tell you how much I cherished—no, loved—you. That even back then, I’d already lost all passion for that ideal. I saw the disgusting truth of humanity—that sooner or later, they’d turn their guns on other species and on each other again. But I kept going anyway. Because I didn’t want to see you sad. Because I wanted you to see the future you dreamed of. But in the end… I became a filthy traitor.”</p>

<p>“You’re a worthy person, Pyrrhos.”</p>

<p>“No, I’m not—damn it—” Each word grew heavier, more labored. Blood stained the white snow red, soaked his uniform, and covered Iglika’s fingertips. She still wouldn’t let go. “I once said I never change, didn’t I? That was a blatant lie. I found a new belief for myself long ago—ever since you disappeared from the partisans. The only thing that never changed… was the feeling I have for you.”</p>

<p>A tear fell, and Iglika leaned down lower. But his trembling hand stopped her.</p>

<p>“Don’t lose your senses now, Commander,” a smile twisted by excruciating pain distorted his handsome features into something grotesque. “If you kiss me now and taste my blood, you’ll become a foolish vampire like me. Don’t you have to live long enough to see your ideals come true? Are you really willing to throw away everything you’ve fought for—just for one stupid, emotional moment, you naive human commander?”</p>

<p>His battle-hardened, ever-cold fingers rose one last time, brushing away the tears on the soft cheek he had always longed for. They were so warm—like an embrace against fingers forever chilled by killing…</p>

<p>“Я люблю тебя… и прости меня, Иглика.” (I love you… and please forgive me, Iglika.)</p>

<p>When that arm fell limp, the last drop of blood flowed from his cooling body—rolling across the white snow, sliding down to the riverbank, dissolving into the icy waters of the Danube… and drifting forever southward.</p>
