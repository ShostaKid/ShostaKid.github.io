<p>"From now on, this will be your room."</p>

<p>Flame Reaver stepped inside before heavily sitting down on a nearby bed. His lifeless eyes scanned the room once before he responded curtly.</p>

<p>"Thanks."</p>

<p>"If you need anything, just call me. I'll always be downstairs."</p>

<p>"Hmm." Flame Reaver mumbled softly in his throat before collapsing onto the bed, turning over and causing the bed to creak slightly as he faced the wall painted a soft blue. "Thanks... a lot."</p>

<p>Cyrene seemed about to say something, but in the end, she just smiled, looked at Flame Reaver one last time, and left the room, closing the wooden door behind her. Alone in the dim room, Flame Reaver turned over on the small bed again, his eyes wandering around. It was a cozy room, not so big that it felt empty, but not so small that it felt stifling. There was a small wardrobe in the corner, and a small table placed right under a large window, allowing the sunlight to stream in and illuminate the softly painted blue room. Everything was neatly and carefully arranged, enough to make even the most demanding person feel comfortable and at ease. But Flame Reaver was different; at this moment, he felt no peace in his heart. The memories, the horrific events he had been through, he remembered them all too clearly. They were etched into his subconscious, so strongly that he never forgot them. And Flame Reaver had accepted that long ago. He didn't try to run from those things, nor did he run from the truth that he was being fiercely consumed by flames from within. Phainon had mercifully forgiven him, but Flame Reaver knew his time was short, and even if those flames didn't burn him from the inside, the things he had experienced would one day lead him to end his own life for self-release.</p>

<p>Flame Reaver turned over once more, his face staring at the soft blue floor. But Cyrene in this world didn't think so. She had agreed to take care of him, to bring him here so he could live with her. Truthfully, Flame Reaver didn't know what to say because he couldn't understand why a person would want to be near him right now. He knew Cyrene wasn't an easy person to read. In fact, even the Cyrene in his own timeline had always done things that were hard to understand, so he wasn't too surprised that the Cyrene in this timeline behaved like his old friend.</p>

<p>Flame Reaver sighed before closing his eyes. A lot had happened, and he wasn't good at thinking, so sleeping was the best option right now. It didn't help him feel calmer or more at peace, but at least it temporarily stopped him from thinking anymore.</p>

<p>---</p>

<p>Flame Reaver awoke with a start, sitting up in the middle of the night. He had it again, the nightmare. It had haunted him for years, and just when he thought he had escaped it, it would pounce again, devouring his subconscious and preventing him from getting a good night's sleep. Flame Reaver wasn't too bothered by the scary things the nightmare brought, because his emotions had been numb for a long time. But the fact that it haunted him every night made it impossible for him to...</p>

<p>As he looked around to calm his mind, Flame Reaver suddenly noticed he was covered by a thin blanket. It made sense; the night here wasn't too cold, but the damp chill always lingered in the air. The only thing was, Flame Reaver distinctly remembered not having a blanket on before he fell asleep. Then, he noticed a small plate of food neatly placed on his bedside table. It was a light, nutritious meal, with a small folded yellow paper next to it. "I know you'll wake up hungry in the middle of the night, so I've prepared a little something for you ^^" the message read, written neatly in red pen on the yellow paper, just readable by the faint light of the large moon shining through the window.</p>

<p>Flame Reaver was no longer human, but that was only in terms of his psychological state. His body was still a biological one, and even though its structure wasn't quite like a normal person's, he still felt hungry like anyone else. His hand trembled slightly as he picked up the fork, and Flame Reaver began to gently cut the piece of beef with the small silver knife in his hand. The meat was cooked perfectly, just the way his late mother used to make it for him. He could taste the sweetness of the meat and the aroma of the dill sprinkled evenly over it. For a moment, not only was the gnawing hunger in his stomach soothed, but something else welled up in Flame Reaver's throat.</p>

<p>But it was all just a fleeting feeling.</p>

<p>---</p>

<p>The singing of a bird on a branch in the morning woke up a still-sleeping Flame Reaver on his bed. He slowly opened his heavy eyes, realizing that the new day's sunlight had already entered his room. As he turned on the wooden bed, his pupils contracted slightly as the bright golden rays penetrated his irises. The large young man leaned on his hand, wearily sitting up. He realized it must be near noon, as the sun was shining diagonally below the window. And when he looked toward the window, Flame Reaver noticed another plate neatly placed on the table, only this time instead of a large piece of perfectly cooked meat, it was two thin slices of bread with ham. Their colors were bathed in the brilliant sunlight, making the sandwiches look even more appetizing. And just like last night, a small piece of paper was placed neatly next to the plate, only this time it was red instead of the soft yellow from the previous night: "Don't skip breakfast, it's not good for you."</p>

<p>Flame Reaver's thin hand picked up the soft sandwich, before he shakily took a bite. The salty taste of the meat quickly stimulated the taste buds on his tongue, awakening a memory deep in his subconscious. It was the sunny days when his mother would wake up early and make him breakfast before leaving the house to work in the fields. The meat was usually saltier than this, because his mother would season it with more salt. Of course, Flame Reaver didn't like that saltiness, and he would often wait for his mother to leave the house before running over to Cyrene's house to get a few glasses of milk to eat with it, and to share his own salty breakfast with his friend.</p>

<p>There was something else on Flame Reaver's bedside table this morning that he only just noticed. It was a book, bound in a hardcover with a vibrant illustration on it. The title "Grimm's Fairy Tales" was printed in sparkling gold on the cover. Flame Reaver recognized it, the illustration of the book he had also bought at Uncle Albion's shop, with money he had secretly taken from his mother, of course. After that came a near-fatal beating, and if Cyrene hadn't come to his rescue in time, Amphoreus might not have had a hero. And it was this very storybook that his pink-haired friend had used to teach Flame Reaver how to read, how to decipher the squiggly words that looked like worms to the white-haired boy back then.</p>

<p>Amidst the waves of memories, Flame Reaver slowly sat down on the wooden bed, his trembling hand turning the pages of the book. The pages, yellowed with time, glowed as the sunlight streamed through the glass window and filled the room with a brilliant light...</p>

<p>---</p>

<p>"Have you been alright lately?"</p>

<p>Phainon's question made Cyrene stop pouring tea, look up at him, and then smile as she replied.</p>

<p>"I thought that should be my question to you, shouldn't it? Is everything still good with the crew? How's Stelle?"</p>

<p>"Oh, yeah. Everything's great, we get to go to a lot of new and interesting places. The world outside Aedes Elysia and Amphoreus really is fascinating." Suddenly, Phainon's ears turned slightly red. With that red color, Cyrene could tell it wasn't from the sun. "And as for my partner... she's fine, and she's still helping me a lot on every journey. I... I really cherish her more and more."</p>

<p>A sad smile briefly appeared on Cyrene's face. But it disappeared so quickly that Phainon didn't even have a chance to see it.</p>

<p>"That's the best thing I could hear from you. You've been through and endured a lot, Phainon. You deserve better things from now on. And I believe Stelle is the person who can bring you happiness," Cyrene said slowly, taking a sip of tea from her porcelain cup.</p>

<p>"Thank you, Cyrene." Phainon unconsciously rubbed his palms together as if to hide his embarrassment. "How about you? Is everything okay?"</p>

<p>"I can't think of a single reason why I wouldn't be okay," Cyrene laughed, and if Phainon had paid close attention, in her laughter, her pink eyes still held a hint of sadness. But of course, the white-haired young man couldn't see it. "Coming back home is truly a feeling beyond words, and my daily activities are slowly returning to their old routine. Everything will soon go back to the way it was, and my life will probably also gradually return to what it was meant to be."</p>

<p>"I'm so glad to hear that. But I wanted to talk about something else. Hmm, you know who I mean..."</p>

<p>"Ah, Flame Reaver, right? Well, don't worry too much about that, Phainon. He's doing fine, even though it seems like he hasn't completely overcome his psychological trauma. But don't worry, I'll always be by his side."</p>

<p>"I know, but that's not what I mean. What I mean is, that guy..." Phainon hesitated for a moment and then said, "That guy isn't a normal person, Cyrene. Even though he's another 'me,' he no longer has the humanity of a normal person. You have to be careful with that Flame Reaver, and if he does anything to you, don't hesitate to call me. I'll make him pay for his actions."</p>

<p>"You're being too negative, Phainon," Cyrene smiled, then poured more tea into the two porcelain cups. "I understand your concern, but don't worry, I'm a careful person. I believe I can help that guy."</p>

<p>"And that's also a way to help me forget you," a thought briefly crossed Cyrene's mind, but she didn't say it out loud.</p>

<p>"I just hope good things happen to you, Cyrene. You've sacrificed so much," Phainon hesitated, as if he had been thinking for a long time before saying this. "And... I also want to apologize to you..."</p>

<p>"Apologize? For what, Phainon?" Cyrene smiled, looking at the white-haired young man who was trying to hide his embarrassment.</p>

<p>"You know what I'm talking about. The thing between you and me..."</p>

<p>A small finger was gently placed on Phainon's lips, making him stop.</p>

<p>"My greatest happiness is seeing you happy with the one you love," Cyrene said softly. "I'm the one who witnessed you grow up, who witnessed you go through so much pain and loss. I understand your good intentions, and maybe I wasn't wrong when..."</p>

<p>Cyrene stopped, she looked down at the floor and tried to avoid the ocean-blue eyes of the white-haired young man. Her small shoulders trembled slightly, and this time, Phainon could see that moment.</p>

<p>"Follow the paths that your heart tells you to. Do what your emotions want you to do. I'm happy when you're with me, but I'll be even happier to see... my friends happy together. Live a happy life with the person you love, my little Snowy."</p>

<p>---</p>

<p>Flame Reaver appeared at the bottom of the stairs just as Cyrene closed the door. He stood still, silently watching the pink-haired girl who was busy clearing the teacups from the table. His presence was only noticed by Cyrene when she turned to face the stairs and saw a nearly two-meter-tall shadow standing in the corner of the dark hallway.</p>

<p>"Phainon," Cyrene called Flame Reaver's name softly. "Do you need something from me? I'm a little busy, but I'll be done soon. If you need something, just wait a minute, okay?"</p>

<p>"...That guy," Flame Reaver's voice was hoarse. "Was he just here?"</p>

<p>"That guy? You mean Snowy? Oh yeah, he was just here. Mainly just to chat with me, since we used to be best friends. Well, saying that, you must have been here for a while, so why didn't you go say hi to 'yourself'?" Cyrene chuckled, teasing Flame Reaver, while still busy clearing the cups and plates on the table.</p>

<p>Without a word, Flame Reaver slowly walked toward Cyrene, his heavy footsteps echoing on the wooden floor. The pink-haired girl noticed it, but before she could turn around and give him a smile like she always did, his large, bony hand touched Cyrene's cheek.</p>

<p>"...He made you sad..." Flame Reaver said softly, his large hand cupping the small girl's cheek.</p>

<p>"No, you're just imagining things, Phainon," Cyrene said with a smile after a moment of silence, her hand touching Flame Reaver's fingers that were still on her cheek, gently squeezing them and looking at Flame Reaver affectionately. "But still, thank you for caring about me like this. I'm really happy."</p>

<p>Suddenly, the pink-haired girl turned around, broke free from Flame Reaver's grasp, and walked toward the kitchen.</p>

<p>"Now let's get ready for dinner. I just got Snowy's recipe for delicious salad, and I'm going to try making it today. Phainon, can you give me a hand?"</p>

<p>After the events that had occurred, Flame Reaver had lost sight in one eye. Even so, with only his one remaining eye, the large young man still saw the trembling of the pink-haired girl's body, the girl who was happily skipping toward the kitchen. They were very small tremors, but Flame Reaver was still able to see them...</p>

<p>---</p>

<p>Flame Reaver slowly walked down the wooden staircase, making it creak softly. Actually, he didn't even know why he suddenly wanted to go see Cyrene. He hadn't even been able to say a single word to her during dinner and had tried to get to his room immediately after the meal was over. Flame Reaver didn't know why, but perhaps it was because after the old memories came rushing back, his mind was now filled with images of his old pink-haired friend. He also didn't know why he had that reaction, because he had really lost all his emotions after so many loops. But what was running continuously through his mind right now?</p>

<p>And also after seeing that tremble. That very slight but clear tremble from Cyrene. As mentioned above, Flame Reaver was not a good thinker. So he couldn't guess why Cyrene in this world felt so sad. Hadn't she met her Phainon? Wasn't that someone she cared about a lot, so why did she look so sad when she met him? Flame Reaver didn't know, and while he was caught up in those unanswerable questions, his feet had stopped in front of the kitchen door without him realizing it.</p>

<p>Hesitating for a moment, the white-haired young man still decided to push the door open. But contrary to what he thought, Cyrene wasn't smiling at him at the wooden table. The girl was still sitting at the dining table, but now her head was slumped on the table, with an open book next to her. The tall young man with the scarred face watched Cyrene for a moment before slowly moving closer. The pink-haired girl breathed in slow, steady rhythms; perhaps she had fallen asleep at the dining table from exhaustion, in her somewhat flimsy pajamas. Flame Reaver hesitated, looking at Cyrene for a moment, seemingly contemplating something. Suddenly, a small hand gently touched Flame Reaver's large fingers, before lightly squeezing them. While the white-haired young man was confused about what to do, a small voice unexpectedly emerged, as soft as a whisper:</p>

<p>"Mom... Mom... Dad... Don't leave me here... I'm scared... I'm so lonely... Phainon, don't leave me... I don't want to be alone... I don't want to... I'm scared..."</p>

<p>In front of Flame Reaver's pupils, which were dilated to their maximum, a single tear, sparkling like a mirror, reflected the flickering candlelight in the kitchen, gently rolled down Cyrene's cheek, and soaked into the wooden table...</p>

<p>---</p>

<p>Cyrene suddenly woke up, emerging from a daze. She realized she was slumped over the wooden dining table, with a book next to her. Maybe she was too tired and had unknowingly dozed off. An owl hooted, signaling that it was past midnight. "Is it late already?" Gently wiping the tear that still lingered on her cheek, Cyrene was about to get up when she realized something was holding her left hand tightly.</p>

<p>Flame Reaver, like a big cat, was slumped on the table, having gently dozed off, his large, bony hand asleep in Cyrene's small fingers. And nearby, next to the book Cyrene was reading, was a small yellow paper, the same kind she used to leave notes for Flame Reaver. On it were lines of not-so-beautifully written but still neat handwriting: "Everything will be okay."</p>

<p>Cyrene unconsciously squeezed his hand tighter, intertwining her fingers with Flame Reaver's. His grip didn't have the warmth of a normal living being, but Cyrene could still feel a different kind of warmth compared to other handshakes. A warmth named "love."</p>

<p>The girl gently leaned in, resting her head on the large body of the white-haired young man, letting her loose strands of hair intertwine with his messy hair. Flame Reaver's large arm, as if by reflex, gently wrapped around the pink-haired girl before pulling her closer to him. The night here was truly cold, but Cyrene could gradually feel the warmth of the young man as their bodies touched. The tears that Cyrene had just wiped away once again flowed down her pink cheeks. The only difference this time was that a smile had formed on her lips. A smile that, though still crooked with sadness, contained a little bit of faith and happiness.</p>

<p>"Yeah, everything will be okay, won't it?"</p>
